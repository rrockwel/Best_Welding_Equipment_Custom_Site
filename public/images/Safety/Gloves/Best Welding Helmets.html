Best Welding Helmets

ESAB Savage A40

      Right off the bat, we have to admit that this helmet has the smallest viewing field of all the helmets that made our list. At 7.7 square inches, it’s viewing area is 20% smaller than that of the Sentinel. At the same time, though, it is the lightest helmet that made the list. While we’re comparing it to the Sentinel, it is also rated at 1/1/1/2 (meaning there is some shading difference depending on the viewing angle), but it generally rings in at less than half the price. Additionally, it doesn’t seem to suffer from some of the lens and sensor issues that the Sentinel can suffer from, depending on the environment you use it in.

      Another benefit of the Savage A40 is the external grind button that also lets you adjust the shade without having to lift your hood. Finally, the Savage runs on solar just like the Sentinel, which can be a benefit or drawback depending on your situation. Either way, it’s something you’ll have to consider based on where you’re welding when taking into consideration the ESAB Savage and the ESAB Sentinel.

      So, overall, the Savage is an absolutely amazing option at the price point. It is rated at the same level as the much more expensive Sentinel, but does have a much smaller viewing field. If you need to work on larger projects where you need to move and look around a lot, this might be an issue for you. If you tend to work on smaller projects, though, it might not. This helmet also offers a low barrier to entry for those just getting into the field, such as welding students or those just starting their first job. If things don't end up working out, you won't break the bank with this option. If everything does work out (as I hope it does), this helmet may take you a ways, but you may have to upgrade at some point.

ESAB Sentinel A50

      One thing that makes this helmet much different than the others is the shape of the main lens. It’s rounded and sits flush with the outside curve of the helmet, making it look great. It’s also functional in that it can help those “berries” bounce off that may otherwise start to build up in a traditionally shaped helmet with a recessed lens. This is great for overhead welding when you are welding straight on.

      On the other hand, the curved lens can cause somewhat of a “fisheye” effect, making it difficult to accurately judge distances that are around the periphery of the viewing area. So, this may not be the best choice for someone who does a lot of welding with their head in a really tight area and needs to weld off to the side of their field of vision frequently. The sensors, unfortunately, are recessed and this can also cause issues with auto-darkening when working far off to the side.

      Another drawback, for those used to working in tight spaces, at least, is the fact that the lens is not recessed at all and sits directly over your head when flipped up. While with a recessed lens helmet the plastic on either side may help mitigate any damage, on this helmet the lens will take the brunt of the impact. So, if you’re used to working in tight spaces, or not worrying when you tap your helmet on something overhead (it is a helmet, after all), this might not be the best choice for you. If, on the other hand, you do a lot of welding straight on, (and don’t hit your head often) this might work out as your favorite helmet so far.

      Another factor that may affect your experience with this helmet is the True Color lenses. They definitely make things brighter and more clear, but may seem too bright for some people, especially anyone who has only used passive lenses.

      One of the best features of this helmet is the digital touch screen. It allows you to program in 8 different presets for different uses (e.g. if you switch between MIG and TIG) so you can just switch from one to the other, without having to fiddle with the settings to get them right. Plus, there's a hard hat adapter option so it can get strapped right to a hard hat, in case you need that functionality.

      Despite the issues some could have with this helmet while working in very tight spaces (consistent with the 2 rating for angle dependence), this helmet has great clarity, is super comfortable, and is a joy to use and wear.
